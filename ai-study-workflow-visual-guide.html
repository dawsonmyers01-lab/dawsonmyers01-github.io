Visual Guide: AI Study Workflow

February 1, 2026

This is the workflow I use when I want to actually learn, not just “read and forget.” The core idea is simple: ask questions, answer them, get feedback, and repeat over time. The visuals below show how I set it up.

1) The loop that makes you learn

If AI is only summarizing, you are probably doing passive studying. I use AI as a tutor and force myself into a loop: question, attempt, feedback, repeat. This is the part that actually builds understanding.

2) Spacing beats cramming

Even short review sessions spread across the week beat one giant cram session. I use AI to generate small daily review blocks so I stay consistent without burning out.

Quick prompt you can steal

Here is the prompt that drives the whole system:

Prompt: Quiz me on [topic]. Ask one question at a time. Wait for my answer. Then tell me what I missed and ask a harder follow-up. After 10 questions, build a 7-day review plan based on my weak spots.

Next week I want to test this workflow on a full unit and track whether my quiz scores improve with less study time.
