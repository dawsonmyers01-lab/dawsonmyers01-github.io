Evidence-Based AI Study System

January 25, 2026

AI can be useful for studying, but only if it pushes real learning. This post is my basic system: retrieval practice first, spaced review second, and productivity scaffolding so I actually follow through.

The two study strategies I trust the most

  • Practice testing (retrieval practice): recalling info from memory improves long-term retention better than rereading.
  • Spaced practice: spreading practice across time improves retention compared to massed practice (cramming).

How I use AI without turning my brain off

Step 1: Tutor loop

I answer first. AI corrects second.

Quiz me on [topic]. Ask 10 questions one at a time. After each answer, tell me what I got right/wrong and why. Increase difficulty if I do well.

Step 2: Target weak spots

No “review everything.” Only what I missed.

Based on my answers, list my top 3 weak spots and give me a 15-minute review plan plus 6 focused questions.

Step 3: Spaced plan

Short sessions across the week.

Create a spaced review schedule for the next 10 days (10–20 minutes/day). Tell me exactly what to review each day and include 3 retrieval questions daily.

References

  • Dunlosky et al. (2013) — high-utility learning techniques
  • Roediger & Karpicke (2006) — testing effect
  • Cepeda et al. (2006) — distributed practice meta-analysis
